Resume

César Salvador is a researcher, professor, and founder specializing in signal processing, acoustics, and immersive technologies. His research has been published in leading journals such as IEEE/ACM TASLP and JASA, achieving an h-index of 8 with over 170 citations. His work has been funded by the Japan Society for the Promotion of Science (JSPS) and the Ministry of Culture of Peru.

César received his M.Sc. and Ph.D. degrees in 2013 and 2016, respectively, from the Graduate School of Information Sciences (GSIS), Tohoku University, Japan. From April 2017 to March 2019, he worked as an Assistant Professor at the Research Institute of Electrical Communication (RIEC), Tohoku University. From August 2019 to January 2021, he worked as Chief Audio Scientist for Silicon Integrated, China.

In August 2019, César founded Perception Research in Peru, to foster education and research on spatial acoustics in the context of multisensory perception, artificial intelligence, and immersive technology. In 2022, his project Memoria Inmersiva got funding from the Ministry of Culture of Peru to continue preserving the immersive soundscapes of historical spaces of Peru.

César is also a Full-Time Professor at the Peruvian University of Applied Sciences (UPC), where he lectures the courses of Physics (UK39) and Computer Science (UK) in the NCUK Program, and the courses of Signals and Systems (EL231), Digital Signal Processing (EL222), Electronic Project 1 (EL236), and Electronic Project 2 (EL237) in the Electrical Engineering Program.

Currently, César is expanding his research to address critical challenges in Peru. At Perception Research, he is developing an intelligent soundscape database that integrates 360 video and 3D audio, enhanced by multimodal language models. At UPC, he is leveraging the accelerometer network of Peru to design algorithms for precise soil and building displacement estimation after earthquakes. These initiatives advance scientific frontiers in immersive technologies and seismic analysis.

Fluent in Spanish, English, Japanese, and French, César is passionate about transdisciplinary and cross-functional collaboration and seeks to contribute his expertise to world-class universities and leading technology firms.
